Autodesk Fusion 360

Guide

This allows one plate to slide through with little to no friction and holds the other plate stationary. We printed off both a left and right clamp and used different distances between the two plates (2mm, 5mm, and 10mm).

Clamps

This holds the two plates together at the top to keep an equal distane between the two plates. We again made different gap distances, 2mm, 5mm, and 10mm.

Calibration Cube

The calibration cube was used to calibrate our camera before we started the experiment. We used it to focus on the mid-way point down the tank between the two plates, which is where we inserted the dye. The one above also includes a syringe holder that was to be hooked up to a syringe pump to allow for an even dispersment of our dye.

Mirror Stand

We decided the best way to take pictures of our experiment was through the bottom of the tank. So we used a first surface mirror and printed the above stand to be angled 45 degrees so we could take pictures between the two plates.
